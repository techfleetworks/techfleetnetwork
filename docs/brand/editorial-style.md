# Tech Fleet — Editorial Style

Baseline: AP Style, overridden by the rules below.

## Brand-specific terminology

| Always                                | Never                       |
|---------------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| Tech Fleet (two words)                | TechFleet                   |
| Team Practices (capitalized framework)| team practices              |
| people who use the platform / members | users (in user-facing copy) |
| constituents                          | clients / beneficiaries     |
| sign in / sign out (verb)             | login (verb)                |
| email                                 | e-mail                      |

The seven Team Practices: Service Leadership, Psychological Safety,
Collective Decision Making, Empowerment, Shared Ownership,
Continuous Improvement, Agility.

## Mechanics

- **Active voice.** "The team finished the project," not "the project was finished."
- **Sentence case** for every heading, button, nav item, and email subject.
- **Singular "they"** — avoid "he/she" and gendered defaults.
- **No "click here"** — link text describes the destination.
- **Capitalize** "Black", "Deaf"; lowercase "white", "blind".
- **Avoid** "guys", "mankind", "crazy", "lame", "dumb", "suffers from".
- **Numbers**: spell out one through nine; numerals for 10+.

## CTAs

Verb + object. "Save draft", "Submit application", "Schedule interview",
"View project". Bare verbs ("Submit", "Save") are forbidden on primary CTAs.
